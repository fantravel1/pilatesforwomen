Full-Body Ignite Session

This 18-minute power session is designed to light up your entire kinetic chain. You will move through a dynamic sequence that weaves together deep core activation, targeted glute work, and fluid athletic transitions. Every exercise is connected to the next through intentional movement patterns that keep your heart rate elevated while maintaining precise Pilates form. Session Breakdown

  • 1
    Dynamic Warm-Up (3 min) Standing roll-down, spinal articulation, cat-cow flow, and hip circles to mobilize the spine and activate the deep stabilizers. Breath synchronization established here sets the tempo for the entire session.
  • 2
    Core Ignition Series (4 min) Hundred variation into single-leg stretch, double-leg stretch, and criss-cross. Continuous flow with no rest between exercises â€” building deep transversus abdominis endurance and oblique power.
  • 3
    Glute Activation Complex (4 min) Bridge series with single-leg lifts, clamshell progressions, and fire hydrant pulses. Targets glute medius and maximus with slow, controlled tempos and isometric holds at peak contraction.
  • 4
    Athletic Transition Flow (3 min) Plank to pike, push-up to downward dog, and bear crawl holds. These transitions train the body to move between positions with control and power â€” building real-world functional strength.
  • 5
    Power Integration (2 min) Teaser progression combined with rolling like a ball and open-leg rocker. These advanced movements integrate the core power and glute strength built throughout the session into full-body coordination.
  • 6
    Cool-Down & Reset (2 min) Spine stretch forward, seated twist, and constructive rest position. Gentle decompression of the spine and parasympathetic nervous system activation to close the session with intention.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Power Flow Pilates session?

A Power Flow session is an 18-minute high-intensity Pilates workout combining dynamic transitions, core-powered movements, and controlled breathing to build functional strength and cardiovascular endurance without impact stress on joints.

How many calories does a Power Flow Pilates session burn?

An 18-minute Power Flow session typically burns 120-180 calories depending on your weight and intensity level. The metabolic afterburn effect means you continue burning calories for up to 2 hours post-session.

Is Power Flow Pilates good for weight loss?

Power Flow Pilates supports weight management through muscle activation, metabolic conditioning, and cortisol regulation. Combined with consistent practice 3-4 times per week, it contributes to sustainable body composition changes.

Can beginners do Power Flow Pilates?

Power Flow includes modifications for every movement. Beginners should start with the foundational tempo, focusing on form precision before adding speed. Most beginners can follow along comfortably within 2-3 sessions.
